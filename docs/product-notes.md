# Haus Product Notes

## Selected P0 Journeys

Haus is centered on four primary journeys:

1. Renovation Concept Pack
2. Accessibility Checker
3. Furniture Fit Planner
4. Designer Pre-Sales Assistant

The Blank Project path remains available for users who want to start from an empty model without journey-specific defaults.

## Positioning

Haus is a concept planning and spatial validation workbench. It turns a floor plan into actionable layout options, checks, and client-ready exports.

Haus is not BIM authoring software, code certification, medical advice, occupational therapy assessment, permit documentation, contractor-ready construction documents, or a substitute for professional site verification.

## Success Metrics

Primary success metric: user reaches a useful applied plan and export in under 10 minutes.

Secondary success metric: user finds at least one actionable spatial risk before buying furniture or starting renovation.

## Research Evidence

### Renovation Concept Pack

Source: https://www.houzz.com/magazine/2025-u-s-houzz-and-home-renovation-trends-stsetivw-vs~181188659

Houzz's 2025 renovation study says 54% of surveyed homeowners renovated in 2024, kitchen and bathroom spending remained material, and 9 in 10 renovating homeowners hired pros. This supports a homeowner-facing concept pack that frames renovation ideas, unresolved assumptions, professional verification needs, and exportable briefs rather than pretending to produce build-ready documents.

### Accessibility Checker

Source: https://www.aarp.org/livable-communities/housing/info-2020/homefit-guide/

AARP's HomeFit Guide positions home safety, aging-in-place, and practical home modification as consumer planning topics. Haus should therefore separate practical guidance from formal code compliance, surface risks such as doorway width, route clearance, bathroom safety, lighting, and trip hazards, and always present accessibility output as planning guidance rather than certification, medical advice, or occupational therapy assessment.

### Furniture Fit Planner

Source: https://www.ikea.com/us/en/planners/

IKEA Kreativ emphasizes realistic, scale-accurate rooms, room scanning, and product selection for living rooms, bedrooms, offices, storage, and decor. This validates a Haus journey focused on dimension provenance, fit checks, product placement, delivery paths, and "buy nothing yet" warnings when plan scale, door widths, or product dimensions are unconfirmed.

### Designer Pre-Sales Assistant

Source: https://pro.houzz.com/for-pros/software-interior-designer

Houzz Pro highlights designer workflows around client proposals, selections, sourcing, product visuals, notes, branding, approvals, budgets, and product libraries. Haus's designer journey should help qualify a client brief, produce clean pre-sales reports, and preserve client-safe language without exposing internal tool logs.

### User-Segment Evidence

Source: https://help.roomsketcher.com/hc/en-us/articles/35581805358877-New-to-RoomSketcher-Start-Here

RoomSketcher identifies homeowners, real estate agents, interior designers, and property professionals as floor-plan users. It also supports starting from an uploaded blueprint, AI conversion, manual tracing, or drawing from scratch, then visualizing in 2D and 3D. Haus should keep both upload-first and manual-tracing flows.

### Branded Furniture And Sharing Evidence

Source: https://home.by.me/en/

HomeByMe pairs 2D floor plans, 3D furnishing, branded product catalogs, 4K images, and sharing for feedback. Haus should keep product provenance visible and make static client-ready exports easy to share without requiring Haus to run.

### AI Home-Design Evidence

Source: https://planner5d.com/

Planner 5D presents AI floor-plan recognition, automated furniture arrangement, shopping-list cost insight, real measurements, and fast 2D/3D planning. Haus should provide deterministic fallbacks, visible measurement assumptions, and AI/LLM badges only when an external provider actually reviews a plan.
