## Positioning

Core message: "Audit-ready public-registry evidence for Singapore client and counterparty diligence."

Dude should not be positioned as an AML/sanctions/legal/tax opinion engine. It should be positioned as:

- a deterministic public-data workflow for Singapore entity diligence;
- a way to preserve provenance, freshness, gaps, limits, and signed export manifests;
- a practical tool for accountants, corporate secretaries, in-house counsel, and legal/compliance operations teams who need repeatable evidence packs.

## Talk Abstract

Title: **From Registry Searches To Audit-Ready Client Diligence**

Abstract:

Singapore teams often perform client and counterparty checks across ACRA, GeBIZ, sector registries, web searches, emails, spreadsheets, and PDFs. The hard part is not finding one record; it is preserving what was checked, when it was checked, what failed, which source was authoritative, and what an analyst still needs to review. This session demonstrates a bounded public-data diligence workflow that turns company/UEN searches into evidence packs with provenance, freshness, gaps, limits, sector follow-ups, PDPA vendor-review prompts, and signed exports. The session is practical and non-advisory: it focuses on better evidence handling, not automated legal, tax, AML, sanctions, or compliance decisions.

Audience:

- corporate service providers;
- public accountants and SMPs;
- in-house counsel and legal operations;
- compliance/operations teams onboarding Singapore counterparties;
- technology and transformation leads in professional services firms.

## Decision

Prioritise ISCA first, then SCCA, then SAL, then ACCA.

Reason: ISCA has the strongest overlap with accounting/corp-services workflows; SCCA has immediate third-party-risk/legal-ops relevance; SAL is strong for legal-tech credibility but likely more competitive; ACCA has relevant accounting members but no current Singapore speaking deadline was visible.
